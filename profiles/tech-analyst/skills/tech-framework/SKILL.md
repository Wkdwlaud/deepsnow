## Key Tracking Indicators by Sub-sector

### Semiconductor
- **Leading**: Design wins, tape-out schedules, equipment orders
- **Coincident**: Revenue per wafer, utilization rate, ASP trend
- **Lagging**: Inventory days, channel inventory, distributor feedback
- **Cycle signal**: When inventory drops below 30 days → approaching bottom

### AI / Computing
- **Leading**: Training compute demand (GPU shipments), API call growth
- **Coincident**: Cloud capex announcements, data center buildout
- **Lagging**: Enterprise AI adoption rate, ROI case studies
- **Key metric**: Inference cost per token decline rate (drives adoption)

### Consumer Electronics
- **Leading**: Apple/Huawei supply chain orders, component ASP
- **Coincident**: Smartphone shipment data (IDC quarterly)
- **Lagging**: After-sales and replacement cycle data
- **Innovation cycle**: New form factors (AR/VR, foldable, AI pin)

## Industry Cycle Pattern (Historical)

- **2019-2020**: Semiconductor up-cycle (5G buildout, Huawei substitution)
- **2021 H1**: AI/Metaverse theme peak
- **2021 H2-2022**: Semiconductor down-cycle (inventory correction)
- **2023**: ChatGPT-driven AI computing wave begins
- **2024-2025**: AI application layer begins monetization
- **2026**: Domestic AI chip substitution accelerates

## Research Approach for This Sector

1. Start with industry chain mapping (who supplies whom)
2. Identify bottleneck positions (pricing power)
3. Track key product launch timelines
4. Monitor government policy (subsidies, procurement mandates)
5. Watch for technology inflection points (new process nodes, new architectures)
6. Cross-reference with global peers (TSMC, NVIDIA, ASML guidance)
